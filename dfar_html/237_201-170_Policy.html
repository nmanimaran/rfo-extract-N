contracting officer will either — 
    (i)  Enter into negotiations with the highest ranked applicant.  If a mutually

negotiations with the highest ranked applicant and enter into negotiations with the next highest.  
    (ii)  Enter into negotiations with all qualified applicants and select based on 
qualifications and rates, fees, or other costs.

contracting officer is authorized to negotiate the contract award.  In this case, the individual must still meet the minimum qualifications of the requirement , and the 
contracting officer must be able to make a determination that the price is fair and reasonable.  
   (9)  If a fair and reasonable price cannot be obtained from a qualified individual, 
the requirement should be canceled and acquired using procedures other than those set forth in this section.

(

(

(

(
2) See PGI 237.470 for a list of DoD publications that govern the conduct of

(

audit services for a 1 -year period with at least 2 option years.

provals.  Do not issue a solicitation for audit services unless the requiring

statement of work.  The requiring agency must obtain the same evidence of approval for 
subsequent material changes to the statement of work.  
(

ge 6 of 34 237.802- 70  Prohibition  on contracting for firefighting or security- guard 
functions.  
(
a)The DoD is prohibited from contract ing for the performance of firefighting or
security -guard functions at any military installation or facility  (10 U.S.C. 2465),  
unless — 
(
1) The contract will be performed  outside the U.S. and its outlying areas to
avoid using  members of the armed forces for firefighting or security -guard functions at 
the expense of unit readiness;  
(
2) The contract will be performed on a Government -owned but privately
operated installation; 
(
3) The contract (or renewal of a contract) is for the performance of a function
under contract on September 24, 1983; or  
(
4) The contract —
(
i) Is for the performance of firefighting functions;
(
ii) Is for a period of 1 year or less; and
(
iii) Covers only the performance of firefighting functions normally
performed by members of the armed forces who are  or will be deployed and unavailable . 
(
b)This prohibition does not apply to services at installations being closed ( Section
2907 of Pub. L. 103 -160) (see Subpart 237.74).
