PGI 229.7002-2 Import duty

The United States shall not pay a contract import duty and contract prices must be exclusive of duty, except when the administrative cost compared to the low dollar value of a contract makes it impracticable to obtain relief from contract import duty. In this instance, the contracting officer must document the contract file with a statement that (a) The administrative burden of securing tax relief under the contract was out of proportion to the tax relief involved; (b) It is impracticable to secure tax relief; (c) Tax relief is therefore not being secured; and (d) The acquisition does not involve the expenditure of any funds to establish a permanent military installation.
