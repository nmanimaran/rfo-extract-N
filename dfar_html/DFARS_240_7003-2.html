240.7003-2 Prohibitions.

(a) Covered products or services prohibition. (1) General. Do not acquire covered products and services, through a contract or subcontract at any tier, from any—

(i) Chinese military company;

(ii) Non-specially designated nationals (SDN) Chinese military-industrial complex company; or Changes are indicated by a change bar in the right-hand margin

(iii) Other covered company.

(2) Exceptions. This prohibition does not apply to—

(i) Components and parts of covered products unless the components and parts are themselves covered products. See PGI 240.7003-2(a)(2)(i).

(ii) Products acquired—

(A) In connection with a visit to the People’s Republic of China by a vessel or an aircraft of the U.S. armed forces;

(B) For testing purposes; or

(C) For the purpose of gathering intelligence.

(b) Entity prohibition. (1) General. Do not award, renew, or extend a contract (e.g., exercise an option) on or after June 30, 2026, with an entity that is identified as—

(i) A Chinese military company operating in the United States; or

(ii) An entity that is subject to the control of a Chinese military company.

(2) Exceptions. This prohibition does not apply—

(i) When awarding, renewing, or extending a contract (e.g., exercising an option) for the procurement of products or services that provides a service that connects to the facilities of a third party, including backhaul, roaming, or interconnection arrangements;

(ii) To contracts awarded prior to June 30, 2026, including when such contracts are modified, extended, or renewed;

(iii) To activities subject to the reporting requirements for intelligence activities of the United States (50 U.S.C. 3091 et seq.), or to any authorized intelligence activities of the United States; or

(iv) To components.

(c) [Reserved]

(d) Prohibition on contracting with entities that contract with lobbyists for Chinese military companies. (1) General. Effective June 30, 2026, do not award a contract with an entity, a parent company of such entity, or a subsidiary of such entity that is a party to a contract with a covered lobbyist.
