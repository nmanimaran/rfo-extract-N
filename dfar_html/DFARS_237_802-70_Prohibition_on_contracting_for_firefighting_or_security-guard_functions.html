237.802-70 Prohibition on contracting for firefighting or security-guard functions

(a) The DoD is prohibited from contracting for the performance of firefighting or security-guard functions at any military installation or facility (10 U.S.C. 2465), unless— (1) The contract will be performed outside the U.S. and its outlying areas to avoid using members of the armed forces for firefighting or security-guard functions at the expense of unit readiness; (2) The contract will be performed on a Government-owned but privately operated installation; (3) The contract (or renewal of a contract) is for the performance of a function under contract on September 24, 1983; or (4) The contract— (i) Is for the performance of firefighting functions; (ii) Is for a period of 1 year or less; and (iii) Covers only the performance of firefighting functions normally performed by members of the armed forces who are or will be deployed and unavailable. (b) This prohibition does not apply to services at installations being closed (Section 2907 of Pub. L. 103-160) (see Subpart 237.74).
