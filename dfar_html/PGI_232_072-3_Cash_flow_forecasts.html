(d)  Cash flow forecasts must  
   (1)  Show the origin and use of all material amounts of cash within the entire 
business unit responsible for contract performance, period by period, for the length of the contract (or until the risk of a cash crisis ends); and    (2)  Provide an audit trail to the data and assumptions used to prepare it.  
  (e)  Cash flow forecasts can be no more reliable than the assumptions on which they 
are based.  Most important of these assumptions are
