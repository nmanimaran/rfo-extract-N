PGI 218.7001-2 Contingency operations

(a)  Selecting appointing, and  terminating the appointment for contracting officers .  See 
part 20 1 and DoD Instruction 5000.6 for waivers to the contracting officer qualification 
requirements pertaining to baccalaureate degrees . 
  (b)  Policy for item unique identification .  Contractors will not be required to provide DoD 
item unique identification if the items, as determined by the head of the contracting activity, are to be used to support a contingency operation.  See part 211.  
  (c)  Use of the Governmentwide commercial purchase card.   Governmentwide 
commercial purchase cards use is not required for purchases valued at or below the micro-purchase threshold if the purchase or payment is for an overseas transaction by a contracting officer in support of a contingency operation, or for training exercises in preparation for overseas contingency, humanitarian, or peacekeeping operations.  See part 
213. 
  (d)  Governmentwide commercial purchase card.  A contracting office supporting a 
contingency operation or a humanitarian or peacekeeping operation may use the Governmentwide commercial purchase card to make a purchase that exceeds the micro-purchase threshold but does not exceed the simplified acqui sition threshold if certain 
conditions are met.  See part 213 . 
  (e)  Imprest funds and third party drafts .  Imprest funds are authorized for use without 
further approval for overseas transactions at or below the micro- purchase threshold in 
support of a contingency operation or a humanitarian or peacekeeping operation.  See part 213. 
  (f)  Standard Form (SF) 44, Purchase Order -Invoice -Voucher .  SF 44s may be used for 
purchases not exceeding the simplified acquisition threshold for overseas transactions by contracting officers in support of a contingency operation or a humanitarian or peacekeeping operation.  See part 213 . 
  (g)  Only one offer.  The requirements at sections 215.371- 2 do not apply to 
acquisitions, as determined by the head of the contracting activity, in support of a contingency operation.  See 215.371- 4(a)(2).  
  (h)  Approval of determination and findings for time- and-materials or labor -hour 
contracts.   The approval requirements in subpart 216. 6 do not apply to contracts that, 
as determined by the head of the contracting activity, support contingency.    
 
 (i)  Undefinitized contract actions .  The head of the agency may waive certain limitations 
for undefinitized contract actions if the head of the agency determines that the waiver is necessary to support a contingency operation or a humanitarian or peacekeeping operation.  See part 217.  
  (j)  Prohibited sources .  DoD personnel are authorized to make emergency acquisitions 
in direct support of U.S. or allied forces deployed in military contingency, humanitarian, or peacekeeping operations in a country or region subject to economic sanctions administered by the Department of the Treasury, Office of Foreign Assets Control.  See part 225. 
  (k)  Authorization Acts, Appropriations Acts, and other statutory restrictions on foreign 
acquisition .  Acquisitions in the following categories are not subject to the restrictions of 225, 
Restrictions on food, clothing, fabrics, specialty metals, and hand or measuring tools:  (1) Acquisitions at or below the simplified acquisition threshold; (2) Acquisit ions outside the 
United States in support of combat operations; (3) Acquisitions of perishable foods by or for activities located outside the United States for personnel of those activities; (4) Acquisitions of food, specialty metals, or hand or measuring tools in support of contingency operations, or for which the use of other than competitive procedures has been approved on the basis of unusual and compelling urgency in accordance with FAR part 6; (5) Emergency acquisitions 
by activities located outside the United States for personnel of those activities; and (6) Acquisitions by vessels in foreign waters.  See part 225.  
  (l)  Electronic submission  and processing of payment requests.   Contractors do not have 
to submit payment requests in electronic form for contracts awarded by deployed contracting officers in the course of military operations, including contingency operations or humanitarian or peacekeeping operations.  See part 232.
