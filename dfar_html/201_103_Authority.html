201.103 Authority

(1)  The defense acquisition system, as defined in 10 U.S.C. 3001(a), exists to 
manage the investments of the United States in technologies, programs, and product support necessary to achieve the national security strategy prescribed by the President pursuant to section 108 of the National Security Act of 1947 (50 U.S.C. 3043) and to support the United States Armed Forces.  
  (2)  The investment strategy of DoD shall be postured to support not only the 
current United States A rmed Forces, but also future A rmed Forces of the United 
States.  
  (3)  The primary objective of DoD acquisition is to acquire quality supplies and 
services that satisfy user needs with measurable improvements to mission capability and operational support at a fair and reasonable price.
